The Emperor's Astrology

紫微斗數 · Zi Wei Dou Shu The tradition behind Purple Star Astrology

This is the background reader for Zi Wei Dou Shu (紫微斗數 zǐ wēi dǒu shù), known in English as Purple Star Astrology. It tells the story of where the system came from, what its Chinese names mean, and how it grew inside the Chinese court over roughly a thousand years. For the interactive chart and the full study, the hub is one step up.

Two systems, one conversation

BaZi and Zi Wei Dou Shu

Both systems emerge from the same cosmological tradition. They answer different questions — and they answer them differently.

八字 · Four Pillars BaZi

The elemental weather of your life — what energies shape your character and era. BaZi reads the five phases active in your birth year, month, day, and hour, revealing the elemental constitution you inhabit.

versus
紫微斗數 · Purple Star Zi Wei Dou Shu

The map of your life — 12 rooms, 100 characters, every decade revealed. ZWDS plots the entire palace of your existence: which rooms are lit, which stars preside, and how the Four Forces move through them over time.

BaZi tells you the weather. Zi Wei gives you the map.

The wider family

Where Zi Wei Dou Shu sits in the family of destiny astrology

Zi Wei Dou Shu (紫微斗數 zǐ wēi dǒu shù) has close relatives. They all start from the same thing: the year, month, day, and hour a person was born. Where they part ways is what they do with that birth moment.

The best known relative is BaZi, the Four Pillars of Destiny (八字 Bāzì, the eight characters). BaZi turns your birth moment into eight characters, two for each of the four pillars, and reads the five elements inside them. It is a reading of balance and elemental weather: which element is strong, which is missing, and how those forces move through your years.

The Korean form of the same Four Pillars method is Saju, in full Saju Palja (사주팔자, romanized sa-ju-pal-ja, the four pillars and eight characters). Saju reads the birth moment the same way BaZi does, through pillars and elements, inside a Korean tradition of naming and interpretation. Read side by side, BaZi and Saju are two languages describing one chart.

Zi Wei Dou Shu takes the same birth moment and does something different with it. Instead of weighing elements, it builds a chart of twelve palaces and places the stars, led by the emperor star 紫微 (zǐ wēi), into those rooms. Where BaZi and Saju read the elemental weather of a life, Zi Wei Dou Shu draws the map of it: which room holds career, which holds marriage, which holds health, and which star presides over each. The Four Pillars tell you what the year is made of. Zi Wei Dou Shu tells you which room you are standing in.

Because they read the same birth moment, these systems are complementary rather than competing. A traditional reader will often cast BaZi or Saju for the elemental picture and Zi Wei Dou Shu for the room by room detail, then let the two readings check each other.

The twelve rooms

The 12 Palaces

Your life is a palace with twelve rooms. Hover any room to reveal its domain — and the reading insight that comes with it.

Palace 01
命宮
The Command Palace
Ming Gong

Core identity, life force, and the overall destiny pattern

Full Domain

The Command Palace is the throne room — the seat of selfhood. The stars here describe your natural authority, your characteristic way of moving through the world, and the shape of your entire destiny arc. It is the first palace a reader turns to.

Reading tip: The star seated in The Command Palace is never neutral. It is always either a strength to build on or a tension to navigate.
Palace 02
兄弟宮
The Peer Circle
Xiong Di Gong

Siblings, peers, cooperative relationships, and equals

Full Domain

The Peer Circle governs the horizontal relationships in your life — siblings by blood, peers by profession, collaborators by choice. Strong stars here signal rich networks; difficult stars reveal rivalry or betrayal within the circle of equals.

Reading tip: This palace also reveals how you function in groups — whether you lead, cooperate, or quietly compete.
Palace 03
夫妻宮
The Mirror of Union
Fu Qi Gong

Marriage, partnerships, intimate bonds, and deep alliances

Full Domain

The Mirror of Union does not merely predict love — it reveals the archetype of your intimate partnerships. The stars here describe the nature of the person you are drawn to and the dynamic that emerges when two lives merge.

Reading tip: The opposite palace — The World Stage — is always in conversation with Union. What you seek abroad, you also seek in a partner.
Palace 04
子女宮
The Legacy Garden
Zi Nü Gong

Children, creativity, lineage, disciples, and living legacy

Full Domain

The Legacy Garden extends beyond children of the body. It encompasses creative output, students you shape, and every project you bring into the world that outlasts you. It is the room where your energy reproduces itself.

Reading tip: Stars here also speak to your relationship with pleasure and spontaneity — the capacity to play.
Palace 05
財帛宮
The Celestial Treasury
Cai Bo Gong

Income, wealth, financial approach, and relationship to abundance

Full Domain

The Celestial Treasury reveals not just how much wealth you attract, but how you relate to money as a force. Some charts earn easily and spend faster. Others are slow to accumulate but protect what they build. The star here names your pattern.

Reading tip: The Flow (化禄) landing in this palace is one of the most sought-after configurations in any chart.
Palace 06
疾厄宮
The Constitution Map
Ji E Gong

Health, body constitution, physical vulnerabilities, and vitality

Full Domain

The Constitution Map is read as a guide to the body's inherent strengths and where vigilance is warranted — not as diagnosis. Traditional readings use this palace to understand energy management, recovery patterns, and constitutional tendencies across the decades.

Reading tip: This palace is always read alongside the health-decade activations. A difficult star here is not fixed fate — it names a terrain to navigate.
Palace 07
遷移宮
The World Stage
Qian Yi Gong

Travel, public persona, success abroad, and life outside the home

Full Domain

The World Stage governs everything that happens when you step beyond the familiar — travel, relocation, public reputation, and how the outside world receives you. Charts with powerful stars here often describe people who shine brightest when they leave home.

Reading tip: This palace sits opposite The Mirror of Union. What you encounter in the world mirrors what you seek in a partner.
Palace 08
奴僕宮
The Alliance Court
Nu Pu Gong

Networks, subordinates, social ecosystem, and those you lead

Full Domain

The Alliance Court maps the web of relationships that exists beneath and around you — employees, supporters, social networks, and the communities that form around your work. It reveals the quality of your loyal base and the risks of betrayal within it.

Reading tip: A chart with The Warrior or The Vanguard here often struggles to delegate — and must learn to trust the court they build.
Palace 09
官祿宮
The Imperial Hall
Guan Lu Gong

Career, vocation, status, authority, and public achievement

Full Domain

The Imperial Hall is where destiny meets ambition. The stars here describe the vocation that is native to you — the work that activates your deepest capacity. In traditional readings this palace also reveals political standing, institutional authority, and the path to recognition.

Reading tip: The Power (化权) transforming a star in The Imperial Hall is one of the signatures of executive leadership.
Palace 10
田宅宮
The Ancestral Foundation
Tian Zhai Gong

Property, home, family roots, real estate, and ancestral inheritance

Full Domain

The Ancestral Foundation covers what is literally beneath your feet — property, domestic environment, family home, and the inheritance (emotional and material) passed down from the generations before you. A strong palace here supports accumulation and stability.

Reading tip: This palace also describes your psychological relationship to home — whether home is a refuge or a source of tension.
Palace 11
福德宮
The Soul Palace
Fu De Gong

Inner life, spiritual fortune, true contentment, and cosmic merit

Full Domain

The Soul Palace is the most interior room in the palace — the chamber of genuine well-being. It describes the quality of your inner life, your capacity for joy, your spiritual orientation, and the invisible reserve of good fortune that sustains you when the outer rooms fall silent.

Reading tip: Advanced readers consider this palace the most important in the entire chart. What good is success in every other room if The Soul Palace is dark?
Palace 12
父母宮
The Origin Gate
Fu Mu Gong

Parents, authority figures, institutional support, and ancestral legacy

Full Domain

The Origin Gate speaks to the conditions of your beginning — the quality of parental relationship, the support (or its absence) from institutions and authority figures, and the ancestral patterns that arrive before you even choose. It also governs documents, credentials, and official standing.

Reading tip: The Origin Gate and The Soul Palace together describe the invisible scaffold of a life — the roots and the sky a person carries within them.
