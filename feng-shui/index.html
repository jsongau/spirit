What feng shui is

風水 (fēng shuǐ) means wind water. Wind moves the vital energy, 氣 (qì); water gathers it. The whole art is arranging a home so the good, living energy, 生氣 (shēng qì), gathers and the harsh energy, 煞氣 (shà qì), is softened. It began with the siting of graves and grew into the placement of homes for the living.

The most-used rule in the house

The commanding position

One idea runs through the whole tradition: a solid mountain, 靠山 (kào shān), at your back and an open bright hall, 明堂 (míng táng), in front. From that seat you see the door without sitting in its line, and nothing important can approach unseen. It sets where the bed, the desk, and the stove should go.

天tiān時shí地dì利lì人rén和hé

Literally heaven's timing, earth's advantage, human harmony

Meaning The three conditions of success align: the right timing, a favorable place, and united people.

Nothing great stands on timing alone, or place alone, or people alone. It stands where the three lean together, and place is the one you can arrange with your own hands.

A proverb we tie to the Year of the Dragon. The pairing is our own reading, not tradition.
