五行, the five phases

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water

The five phases are moving processes, not fixed things, and they are how feng shui strengthens or calms a room.

The cycles

How the phases feed and restrain each other

In the generating cycle, 生, Wood feeds Fire, Fire makes Earth, Earth bears Metal, Metal carries Water, and Water nourishes Wood. To strengthen a weak phase, add the one that generates it. In the controlling cycle, 克, Wood parts Earth, Earth dams Water, Water quenches Fire, Fire melts Metal, and Metal cuts Wood. To calm an excessive phase, add its controller, or, more gently, the phase it generates, which drains it.

At home

Colors, shapes, and materials

  • Wood 木. Greens and teals, tall shapes, timber and plants; east and southeast.
  • Fire 火. Reds and oranges, pointed shapes, candles and real light; south.
  • Earth 土. Yellows and terracotta, flat squares, ceramic and stone; center, northeast, southwest.
  • Metal 金. Whites and metallics, round shapes, metal and stone; west and northwest.
  • Water 水. Deep blues and black, wavy shapes, glass and reflective surfaces; north.

上shàng善shàn若ruò水shuǐ

Literally the highest good is like water

Meaning The finest virtue is like water, which benefits all things and flows to the low places without contending.

Water asks for nothing and shapes everything. It sinks to the low room others avoid and turns even that into a home. To be strong like water is to stop contending and still reach everywhere.

A proverb we tie to the Year of the Rat. The pairing is our own reading, not tradition.
