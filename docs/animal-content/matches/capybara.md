# Who the Capybara grows with
_Your trine is the Rabbit and the Goat, your secret friend the Tiger, and the Snake is the teacher you will clash with._

## Sea Otter · trine (Rabbit) · Deep Recognition
Your San He ally and your kin in the water. Both of you are Cancer Water, and both of you survive by staying together. Sea otters hold hands while they sleep so the current cannot drift them apart, wrap themselves in kelp to stay anchored, and raft in groups of dozens. That is your instinct exactly — the herd on the bank, nobody left to float off alone. What the otter gives you is proof that softness is a strategy, not a weakness: it cracks shells with a stone kept in a pocket of skin, tender and resourceful at once. What you give it is stillness — a wider, calmer bank to raft against. Two warm-blooded water creatures who both know the whole point of a group is that no one drifts.

## Kakapo · trine (Goat) · Strong
Your San He ally from the air side of the wheel. The kakapo is a flightless, nocturnal parrot — round, heavy, gentle, famous for wandering right up to people with no fear at all. It is one of the most trusting animals alive, a thing you understand from the inside. The trine gives you an easy, unforced warmth; the only gap between your Water and its Air is that it lives in its own head a little more than you do. What the kakapo gives you is delight — pure, odd, undefended company. What you give it is safety, the calm bank it can be strange on without being hurt for it. Two soft, unguarded creatures, both a little too open for the world — which is exactly why they are good for each other.

## Moon Bear · secret friend (Tiger) · Strong
Your Liu He secret friend, and your quiet opposite in company. The moon bear wears a pale crescent on its chest, climbs high into trees, and builds itself a nest of bent branches to rest in alone. It is solitary where you are a whole herd — but it shares your Cancer Water heart, the same need for a den that truly holds. This is the bond that asks little and gives shelter. What the moon bear gives you is depth: proof that some warmth is private, kept in a nest above the ground, and no less real for being unshared. What you give it is the opposite invitation — a bank where being near others costs nothing at all. The host and the hermit, secretly built from the same water.

## Chambered Nautilus · clash to navigate (Snake) · Hard Teacher
Your Liu Chong opposite, and the mirror that unsettles. The nautilus builds its life one sealed chamber at a time, filling each emptied room with gas to stay afloat, and rising from the deep only in the dark. It is the hermit of the wheel — every door closed behind it, a last room no one enters. You are its reverse: the most open creature on the bank, birds on your back, no closed room at all. This clash asks the most and can give the most. The nautilus can teach you that a door you are allowed to close is not a betrayal of your warmth, and you can teach it that an unlocked bank is not the danger it fears. Move gently. When two patient, tender souls actually do the work, it becomes the most honest bond either one ever builds.
