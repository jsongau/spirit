# Who the Andean Condor grows with
_Your trine is the Ox and the Snake, your secret friend the Dragon, and the Rabbit is the teacher you will clash with._

## Shoebill · trine (Ox) · Deep Recognition
Your San He ally and the closest thing to your own reflection on the wheel. Both of you are Capricorn Earth; both of you were built to wait. The shoebill stands motionless in the swamp for hours, a grey statue over the water, until the one lungfish it wanted finally surfaces — then it strikes, all at once. You do the same in the sky, holding the ground until the thermal is real. What the shoebill gives you is proof that your stillness is not idleness but a discipline as old as yours. What you give it is scale — the reminder that patience can be spent on a hundred-mile horizon, not just the next meal. Two who never move early rarely have to explain themselves to each other.

## Tuatara · trine (Snake) · Strong
Your other San He ally, and the one who slows your clock even further. The tuatara is a living fossil, near-unchanged for two hundred million years, with a heartbeat so slow it can go minutes between breaths and a life that runs past a century. It matures on nobody's schedule but its own. You share the Capricorn Earth patience, but you burn yours in the sky; the tuatara burns it lying still in the cool. What it gives you is permission to take the long view further still — decades, not seasons. What you give it is altitude, a horizon it will never climb to but likes knowing is there. Between you, nothing is urgent, and almost nothing is lost.

## Bearded Vulture · secret friend (Dragon) · Deep Recognition
Your Liu He secret friend, and the only other giant who lives where you live. The bearded vulture rides the same high updrafts, nests on the same cliffs, and makes its living on what remains — it is the one bird that eats bone, carrying it aloft and dropping it on rock to crack it open. You clean the plains; it cleans the peaks. This is the quiet bond that asks nothing and simply holds. What it gives you is company at an altitude almost nothing else can reach. What you give it is the same — a second immense, unhurried presence that reads the mountain wind exactly the way you do. Two creatures of the high, cold air, neither one lonely.

## Fennec Fox · clash to navigate (Rabbit) · Hard Teacher
Your Liu Chong opposite, and the fastest thing on your list. The fennec is everything you are not: tiny, quick, all nerves and enormous ears, awake in the dark, reacting to a sound before it has even finished. You are slow, huge, and daylit, moving only when the air is right. Where you wait for the current, it is already three moves ahead. This bond stings because it makes your patience look like paralysis and its speed look like panic — from each side. But the fennec can teach you to act before the perfect thermal arrives, and you can teach it that not every sound needs answering. Move slowly toward it. What the desert's quickest and the sky's most patient build, when they actually try, neither could build alone.
