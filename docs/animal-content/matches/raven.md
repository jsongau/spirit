# Who the Raven grows with
_Your trine runs to the Rat and the Dragon, your secret friend is the Snake, and the Tiger is the clash you learn from._

## Meerkat · trine (Rat) · Deep Recognition
The Rat to your Monkey, and a fellow air mind, so you read each other at a glance. The meerkat lives the way you think: as a system. One stands sentinel on its hind legs while the rest forage, and its alarm calls are specific, a different sound for a hawk than for a snake, information passed clean and fast across the whole group. That is your systemic map made into a living lookout. It gives you the value of the shared watch, of intelligence that protects a group instead of only outrunning it. You give the meerkat reach and depth, a longer game than the next threat on the horizon. You both survive by seeing first and telling the others. Between you, nothing sneaks up.

## Humpback Whale · trine (Dragon) · Workable
The Dragon to your Monkey, a real trine, but your air and its water do not naturally mix, and that is the work. The humpback is everything you are not. It feels at a volume that fills an ocean, sings songs that take a season to finish, and leaves its own path to shelter a life that is not its own. You calculate; the whale simply feels, at scale. It gives you weight, a reason to land inside a moment instead of mapping it from above, a pull back toward the body of things. You give the whale foresight and wit, a way to aim that enormous feeling before it buries the point. You will never process the world the same way. But the whale can carry what you plan, and you can sharpen what it feels.

## Chambered Nautilus · secret friend (Snake) · Strong
Your Liu He friend, the quiet pact, though your restless air meets its deep water and has to slow down to hear it. The nautilus is patience itself, a living fossil that builds its shell one sealed chamber at a time and floats on the emptied past, reading the dark by pressure instead of sight, rising only at night. Where you cache for a future you keep rehearsing, the nautilus simply seals the last room and lets it hold the weight. It gives you stillness, a way to keep the past without picking it back up, and the knock that waits instead of maneuvers. You give the nautilus a wider sky, news from the whole board it never surfaces to see. The Snake and the Monkey, the reader of pressure and the reader of the room, quietly on the same side.

## Harpy Eagle · clash to navigate (Tiger) · Friction
The Tiger to your Monkey, the sign set against you, though your air feeds its fire and keeps the clash from turning cold. The harpy eagle is raw force where you are pure calculation. It does not model the social field or cache for later; it sits in the canopy and drops on the monkey with talons the size of a bear's claws, and the exchange is over before a plan could form. It is, in the plainest sense, what hunts a creature like you. That is the teaching. The harpy shows you the limit of foresight, the moment when the room does not care how many moves ahead you saw. It gives you respect for direct power. You give it a mind it cannot grab, always one branch further than the strike. Learn from the talon without living in fear of it.
