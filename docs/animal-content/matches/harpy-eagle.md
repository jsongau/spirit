# Who the Harpy Eagle grows with
_Your trine runs to the Horse and the Dog, your secret friend is the Pig, and the Monkey is the one who teaches you._

## Orca · trine (Horse) · Strong
Two apex hunters, one of the canopy, one of the cold sea. The orca rules its water the way you rule your trees, at the very top of everything, and it kills by planning, by coordinated silence that ends in one decisive move. You recognize the method instantly, because it is yours: hold still, read the situation, close the distance once. What the orca gives you is the pod, the reminder that even a sovereign predator hunts better with others, that your solitude is a choice and not a rule. What you give the orca is patience for the single strike, the discipline to wait in stillness rather than run the whole ocean down. Fire and water make steam where they meet, so this runs warm and a little volatile, but Tiger and Horse are true trine. Strong, and respectful on both sides.

## African Grey Parrot · trine (Dog) · Deep Recognition
This one surprises you. The African grey is everything you are not: loud, quick, endlessly verbal, a mind that names things and remembers who said what. Where you hold silence as power, the parrot holds voice as connection, and the Dog's loyalty runs under all of it. What the parrot gives you is language for a presence that usually just looms, a way to be recognized before you have to move, so the crown gets seen without the strike. What you give the parrot is stillness, a center that does not need to fill every silence, proof that some things are said best by not saying them. Fire and air feed each other cleanly. Tiger and Dog are trine, and this is the rare bond where opposite instincts complete instead of collide. Deep recognition, loud and quiet at once.

## Golden Snub-nosed Monkey · secret friend (Pig) · Strong
The Pig is the Tiger's secret friend, and this is the warmth your kingdom is missing. The golden snub-nosed monkey survives some of the coldest forest winters in the world by refusing to be alone in them, living in bands hundreds strong, huddling through the snow, sharing heat because none of them would last it apart. You, the solitary sovereign, watch that and feel something loosen. What the monkey gives you is company that costs you no authority, warmth without surrender, a crowd that would never ask you to stop being the largest thing in it. What you give the monkey is a guardian at the edge of the trees, a presence that makes the whole band safer just by being near. Both of you run on fire. Secret friends, and this one thaws you.

## Raven · clash to navigate (Monkey) · Friction
Here is the trickster who will not be ruled. The raven is as smart as anything with feathers, a solver of puzzles, a keeper of grudges, a bird that plays for the sake of playing and tests every boundary just to watch it bend. You hold the center by force of presence. The raven undermines every center it finds, on principle. The Tiger and the Monkey sit across the wheel, and the clash is real: your silence reads to it as something to poke, and its constant cleverness reads to you as noise that will not settle. But fire and air do feed each other, so this friction sparks instead of smothering. What the raven teaches you is that authority untested goes brittle. What you give it is the one thing it cannot outwit and secretly wants, a still, immovable center. The teacher you argue with for years.
