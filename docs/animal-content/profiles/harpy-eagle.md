# The Harpy Eagle — The Silent Sovereign
**Leo × Year of the Tiger · Wood**

You are the largest thing in your world, and the hardest to spot before you move.

## The crossing
Leo gives you a presence too large to blend into any background, the instinct to hold the center of the territory rather than its edge, and a standard for what a kingdom is supposed to look like. The Tiger gives you the discipline to go still and let a threat pass beneath notice, a guarded nature that answers to almost no one, and the nerve to close a distance in one uninterrupted motion. Cross them and you get a creature that is the biggest thing in its world and still the hardest to see coming. The size is real. The silence is real. The Tiger keeps Leo's presence from announcing itself before it is ready, and Leo keeps the Tiger's guardedness from shrinking into something small.

Where they pull against each other: Leo wants the crown seen, the Tiger wants the strike unseen until it lands. Fused well, that is discipline, and you let your size announce itself once, only when it counts. Fused badly, the stillness curdles into hiding, and a presence built to be unmistakable spends its whole life unnoticed by choice.

## In the wild
The real harpy eagle rules the canopy of the Neotropical rainforest, one of the most powerful raptors alive, with talons the length of a bear's claws. It does not soar and circle for show. It perches, still and silent for hours, reading the branches below, then drops through the leaves and takes a monkey or a sloth in a single fall. Short broad wings, built to move through trees rather than above them.

## How you move
Your gift is a power that never has to raise its voice. Your edge is the temptation to disappear entirely. Stay still, but do not confuse silence with absence.

_Meaning, not prediction._
