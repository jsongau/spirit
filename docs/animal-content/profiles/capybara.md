# The Capybara — The Common Ground
**Cancer × Year of the Pig · Water**

You never call anyone over. They come anyway, and they stay.

## The crossing
Cancer gives you the instinct to make a space that holds people instead of impressing them, and a memory for how they felt, not just what they said. The Pig gives you a warmth that arrives without calculation and real pleasure in company you do not need to be the center of. Fused, you become the gathering point that does not know it is one — you do not recruit, do not perform, do not manage. You simply exist in a way that makes others want to stay.

Where they pull against each other: Cancer builds home from attachment and notices exactly who is missing; the Pig keeps extending the welcome without tracking who has taken. One half wants to know who is really there. The other never stops holding the door.

## In the wild
The capybara is the largest rodent alive and the calmest animal on the riverbank. Birds land on its back to pick off ticks, monkeys and turtles rest against it, and almost anything is allowed to share its water. It grazes in a loose herd near the river and slips into the water when danger comes, but its whole shape is an invitation — the one warm bank the whole floodplain leans on. It hosts without ever seeming to decide to.

## How you move
Your gift is a presence that makes people stay. Your edge is a welcome so wide it forgets to count. Keep the door open — but save one place at the water for the few who were actually there when it mattered.

_Meaning, not prediction._
