# The Andean Condor — The Unhurried Immensity
**Capricorn × Year of the Rooster · Metal**

You cross a hundred miles on a single held breath, and you never once look like you are trying.

## The crossing
Capricorn gives you the willingness to climb for a height that only pays off after a long, unglamorous wait — one real structure worth more to you than a dozen options. The Rooster gives you a presence too large to hide, and a body that reads a room's judgment before a word is said. Fused, they make a creature built for enormous scale that never mistakes scale for noise. You wait for the true current, then arrive fully visible and exactly on time.

Where they pull against each other: Capricorn wants to wait for real lift, the Rooster wants to be seen now. The people who last learn that your stillness is not indifference — it is you, reading the air.

## In the wild
The Andean condor is the largest flying bird alive, a ten-foot wingspan that beats barely one percent of the time. It reads thermals and the ridge's rising wind and covers a hundred miles without a single flap, waiting on the ground until the air is right. It roosts on high cliffs, lives past sixty, and raises one chick every couple of years. Nothing about it is wasted, and nothing about it is quick.

## How you move
Your gift is distance that costs you almost nothing. Your edge is the wait that looks, from below, like you have quit. Trust the height you were built for — but tell the ones on the ground what your stillness means, before they mistake it for leaving.

_Meaning, not prediction._
