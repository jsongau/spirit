# Reading Order, Ethics, and Uncertainty Language — teach-back research

## Concept in one paragraph

紫微斗數 (Purple Star Astrology) is taught in the responsible tradition as a life schedule, not a verdict. The chart is fixed at birth, but the corpus separates 命 (the constitutional pattern set at birth) from 運 (the rotating luck of the decade and the year), and it is the interaction of the two, together with the person's own choices, that produces a life. A reader's job is to describe structure, timing, and the recurring lesson a domain tends to carry, then hand the choice back to the person sitting there. The classical stars and the 三方四正 (three-directions-four-positions) cross-reading method are inherited technique. The idea that a reader "may never say" certain things, and that a session should follow a set shape, is closer to professional ethics carried in specific teaching lineages than to a single classical rulebook, so this teach-back frames the consultation structure as house method rather than ancient law.

## The reading order and evidence hierarchy (what outranks what)

Across the teaching guides the order is remarkably consistent, and it moves from the whole grid inward, never from one isolated box outward.

1. **Read the grid as one object first.** Every guide warns against reading a single palace alone. The chart is a square of twelve palaces around an empty centre that holds the birth data, and the palaces are meant to be synthesised together.
2. **Anchor on the 命宮 (Life Palace).** This is treated as the base palace: the main stars there, their brightness, and any transformation they carry describe the constitutional identity that everything else is read against.
3. **Read the power axis, not the point.** The teaching phrase is 「新手看單宮，高手看三方四正」, roughly "a beginner looks at one palace, an adept looks at the three-directions-four-positions." For any palace you weigh four boxes together: the palace itself, its opposite palace, and the two trine palaces four positions away. For the 命宮 that means 命宮 plus 遷移宮 (opposite), 財帛宮, and 官祿宮.
4. **Weight those four boxes unequally.** The home palace carries the most say, the opposite palace is next, and the two trine palaces support. One andylifemap teaching uses a cabinet-of-government image: the home palace is the leader, the opposite palace is the executive, the trine palaces are the other ministries.
5. **Then layer timing.** Only after the natal structure is set do you bring in the 大限 (decade) and 流年 (year). The natal stars never move; the decade and year act as a moving lens that re-reads the same fixed stars.
6. **Let the 四化 (four transformations) point the way.** The birth-year stem generates 化祿, 化權, 化科, 化忌, and these are described as the direct cause and engine of events rather than extra stars. Practically, readers are told to check where 化忌 lands, in both the natal and the decade layer, because that palace marks the domain under the most pressure and, in the teaching frame, the domain with the most growth available.

Two tie-breakers sit inside this: star brightness can outrank position (a strong star in the opposite palace can outweigh a weak star in the home palace), and the same star read at 陷 (fallen) brightness inverts toward its shadow expression rather than its clean one. Different schools split here too. The 三合 (San He) school leans on star combinations and brightness, while the 四化 (Si Hua) school leans on the flying transformations, and the same chart is read differently depending on which lens leads.

## Handling conflicting signals honestly

Conflict is the normal state of a chart, not a defect in it, and the method is built to hold it rather than resolve it away.

- **A soft home palace with a forceful trine is not a contradiction to explain away.** The worked teaching example is a gentle 命宮 sitting with a very strong 財帛宮: the honest reading is that the person is mild in general but turns assertive around money, and that money moves their emotions more than most things. You report both signals and the switch between them, you do not average them into one bland claim.
- **Layers can pull against each other, and you say so.** When natal, decade, and annual 四化 converge on one palace the effect is strongest; when they conflict, the reader names the internal contradiction between a long-term pattern and a short-term push rather than picking a winner.
- **Absence of confirmation lowers confidence.** If a promising home palace is undercut by a difficult opposite palace, the teaching is that the promise may not reach the outside world cleanly, so a single bright box is not a conclusion until the surrounding three agree.
- **State your evidence out loud.** Responsible framing sounds like "the structure suggests X, but the opposing palace argues against it, so I hold this loosely." When the signals will not reconcile, the correct answer is that the chart is showing tension in that area, not a false certainty.

## The uncertainty phrasebook (what a reader may say and may never say)

**A reader may say:**
- "The structure suggests..." and "this palace tends toward..."
- "This domain carries the recurring lesson of..." (especially where 化忌 lands)
- "Different schools read this differently" (for example San He brightness versus Si Hua flying stars)
- "This is a period that asks for extra care around..." paired with practical, ordinary-life suggestions
- "The chart cannot tell us that" for anything outside what the method actually shows
- "This is one factor among many, and the choice stays with you"

**A reader may never say:**
- Any fixed date or verdict of death, or a claim to read lifespan. The named lineage teaching is explicit that people meet hardship but the moment of death is not cleanly visible, that it depends on far too many outside factors to pronounce, and that raising it at all, right or wrong, harms the listener.
- A deterministic illness or disaster claim. Where a period looks demanding, the guidance is to translate it into lifestyle care, rest, and ordinary check-ups, not a diagnosis.
- A guaranteed wealth or poverty outcome. Longevity and fortune are to be discussed as "possibilities and conditions," framed as symbolic hints from combined factors.
- A marriage-or-divorce instruction. The tradition lists advising someone to break up or divorce as off-limits; you describe what the relationship palace shows plainly and return the decision to the person.
- Anything the client did not ask about. The 不問不答 ("do not answer what was not asked") principle, tied to a 荀子 passage, holds that dumping unrequested heavy news on an unprepared person is itself the harm. The reader keeps a detached, scholarly posture and lets the client decide how much they want to know.

The through-line is that the reader returns 主體性 (agency) to the client. The chart informs a choice, it does not make one.

## A responsible consultation structure (labeled house method)

The following shape is our own responsible framing for the academy. It draws on how the lineages describe a good reading, but presenting it as a fixed five-step protocol is house method, not a claim of classical authority.

1. **Frame and consent.** State plainly what 紫微斗數 is and is not: a life schedule and a decision aid, not fortune sealed shut. Ask what the person actually wants to look at, and confirm the off-limits topics up front.
2. **Establish structure.** Read the 命宮 and its 三方四正 to describe constitution and character before touching any prediction.
3. **Locate the present.** Bring in the active 大限 and 流年 and the 化忌 of each layer to show where the current lesson and the current pressure sit.
4. **Report signals honestly, including conflict.** Give the strong reads, the counter-reads, and your confidence in each. Name tensions instead of smoothing them.
5. **Hand back the choice.** Close with ordinary, actionable suggestions and an explicit reminder that the decision belongs to the client. Answer only what was asked.

## Common student errors (3-5, each one sentence)

- Reading one palace in isolation and calling it a conclusion, instead of weighing the full 三方四正.
- Treating 化忌 as doom rather than as the arrow pointing to the domain that most needs attention and offers the most growth.
- Averaging conflicting signals into one vague statement rather than reporting both signals and the switch between them.
- Skipping straight to timing predictions before the natal structure has been established.
- Volunteering heavy claims about death, illness, or a relationship that the client never asked about, which breaks the 不問不答 principle.

## Model teach-back answer — beginner register (2-4 sentences)

紫微斗數 works like a life schedule, not a sentence. You never read one box on its own; you look at the palace together with the three others it connects to, which is called the 三方四正, and you weigh the main palace most heavily. A good reader says "the structure suggests" and "this area tends to carry this lesson," and never gives you a fixed claim about death, illness, or who to marry. The chart is meant to inform your choice, and the choice always stays with you.

## Model teach-back answer — practitioner register (2-4 sentences)

I anchor on the 命宮, read its 三方四正 with the home palace weighted first, the opposite palace second, and brightness allowed to override position, then I layer the 大限 and 流年 and follow the 化忌 in each layer to locate the working lesson. Where natal, decade, and annual signals converge I read the effect as strongest, and where they conflict I name the contradiction rather than resolving it prematurely, because different schools, San He on brightness and Si Hua on the flying transformations, would already read the same board differently. I keep to 不問不答, answer only what is asked, and translate any demanding period into ordinary lifestyle care rather than a verdict. Death timing, guaranteed fortune, and break-up instructions stay outside the reading, and I hand every decision back to the person in front of me.
