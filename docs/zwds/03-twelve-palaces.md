# The 12 Palaces of Zi Wei Dou Shu (紫微斗數)
## A Comprehensive Reference

## Foundational Context

The ZWDS chart consists of twelve fixed palace positions (十二宮, shí'èr gōng), each anchored to one of the twelve Earthly Branches (地支), forming a permanent grid into which over 100 named stars are distributed based on birth data. The system's most important interpretive principle is that no palace is read alone — each must be evaluated in context of its companion palaces, the stars within them, and the four Transforming Stars (四化, Si Hua) that activate specific areas of life.

---

## THE SAN FANG SI ZHENG SYSTEM (三方四正) — Palace Courts

Before individual palace descriptions, the court system must be understood because it defines how palaces are read holistically.

The phrase 三方四正 (San Fang Si Zheng) means "Three Directions and Four Cardinal Positions." Every palace has three companion palaces that form a court of four: the palace itself, its direct opposite (對宮), and two palaces that form a triangle at 120-degree intervals.

**The court system is based on the four Earthly Branch groupings:**
- **Court 1 — Cardinal Cross:** Zi (Rat), Wu (Horse), Mao (Rabbit), You (Rooster)
- **Court 2 — Birth Positions:** Yin (Tiger), Shen (Monkey), Si (Snake), Hai (Pig)
- **Court 3 — Tomb Positions:** Chen (Dragon), Xu (Dog), Chou (Ox), Wei (Goat)

Within each court, the four palaces are read together simultaneously. The palace directly opposite (對宮) has the strongest single relationship — it is essentially the mirror or shadow of the primary palace.

Classical texts emphasize that a weak primary palace can be compensated by strong opposite and triangular palaces, while a palace that looks strong in isolation can be undermined by malefic stars in its three companion positions.

### The Primary Court — Life-Career-Wealth-Travel (命財官遷)

The most important court grouping in any chart reading is the **Life-Career-Wealth-Travel quadrant**: Life (命宮), Career (官祿宮), Wealth (財帛宮), and Travel (遷移宮) palaces must always be assessed together to judge the overall life trajectory.

### Opposite Palace Pairs (對宮)

| Palace | Opposite Palace |
|--------|----------------|
| Life (命宮) | Travel/Migration (遷移宮) |
| Siblings (兄弟宮) | Servants/Network (奴僕宮) |
| Spouse (夫妻宮) | Career (官祿宮) |
| Children (子女宮) | Property (田宅宮) |
| Wealth (財帛宮) | Health (疾厄宮) |
| Fortune/Virtue (福德宮) | Parents (父母宮) |

## THE FOUR SI HUA TRANSFORMATIONS IN PALACES

- **化祿 Hua Lu:** The life domain of that palace enters a state of beneficial ease and resource arrival
- **化権 Hua Quan:** The life domain acquires increased initiative, assertion, and control
- **化科 Hua Ke:** The life domain gains the quality of being recognized, publicized, and respected
- **化忌 Hua Ji:** That palace carries the recurring lesson or pressure point — the deepest karmic work lives here. Not cursed — it is where the most conscious, careful engagement is required, and where deepest growth occurs.

**Hua Ji effects by palace:**
- Life Palace: Chronic constitutional challenges; must work harder to fulfill potential
- Siblings: Conflicts with peers, family fragmentation
- Spouse: Relationship karma; late marriage, difficult partnerships, multiple unions
- Children: Fertility challenges or strained parent-child bonds
- Wealth: Financial worry, leakages, losses
- Health: Chronic health vulnerabilities; recurring physical adversity
- Travel: Misfortune abroad; difficult external reputation
- Network: Betrayal from friends or employees
- Career: Career obstacles, conflicts with authority
- Property: Property disputes, unstable housing, domestic disharmony
- Fortune/Virtue: Inner restlessness, chronic anxiety, spiritual seeking from emptiness
- Parents: Difficult parental relationships, authority issues, contract problems

## THE 12 PALACES — FULL REFERENCE

### 1. 命宮 Ming Gong — Life Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Identity Court / The Command Palace / Destiny Seat
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 1st House (Ascendant/Rising)

**Domain:** Core identity, innate temperament, physical constitution, intellectual capacity, moral nature, physical appearance, and the overall pattern of fortune or misfortune across the entire lifetime. Determines leadership potential and the degree of natural blessing or hardship embedded in the destiny.

**Beyond the surface:** The Life Palace does not simply describe personality — it reveals the quality of a person's entire life force (命). Classical texts assess it to determine longevity range, whether prosperity or struggle will be the dominant life theme, and whether the person has the inner resources to overcome adversity. Described as "the emperor's throne in the cosmic court."

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star (紫微) in this palace is considered the most auspicious placement. The rule of thumb: assess whether stars create a "ministers meeting at court" pattern (harmonious command structure) or a "lone king with no court" situation (potential for arrogance and isolation).

**Opposite palace:** Travel Palace (遷移宮) — the outer persona one projects in the world beyond home territory. Together, Life and Travel reveal the gap or alignment between inner self and public presentation.

**Multi-palace reading requirement:** Must be read alongside Wealth, Career, and Travel palaces at minimum. Also read alongside Body Palace (Shen Gong) — the marker that shows where life becomes most embodied through action.

### 2. 兄弟宮 Xiong Di Gong — Siblings Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Peer Circle / The Inner Council
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 3rd House (siblings, immediate community, communication)

**Domain:** Brothers, sisters, cousins, close friends who function as siblings, peers of equal standing, cooperative horizontal relationships. Reveals number and gender of siblings, whether family bonds remain strong, whether siblings will be helpful or competitive.

**Beyond the surface:** This palace also speaks to business partners of equal standing, close friends made in youth, and the quality of cooperation vs. rivalry one attracts in non-hierarchical contexts. The Fortune Palace (福德宮) must be examined first — its condition determines whether siblings stay united or drift.

**Reading stars here:** Southern Dipper stars favor more male siblings; Northern Dipper stars favor female. Heavenly Minister (天相) suggests siblings from different mothers.

**Opposite palace:** Career Palace (官祿宮) — the state of one's peer relationships directly echoes the support structure around one's career.

---

### 3. 夫妻宮 Fu Qi Gong — Spouse Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Mirror of Intimate Union / The Karmic Partnership Chamber
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 7th House (partnerships, marriage, open enemies)

**Domain:** Marriage, long-term romantic partnerships, the character and qualities of the spouse or primary partner, the timing of marriage, fidelity patterns, the quality of daily conjugal life, and the karmic nature of the intimate bond.

**Beyond the surface:** The Spouse Palace functions as a "karmic mirror" — the qualities of the stars found here often describe qualities the person either seeks, projects, or must learn to integrate through relationship. A person's capacity for 100-year companionship ("白頭偕老") is read here. Extramarital tendencies ("horizontal flute rumors") appear through specific star combinations.

**Reading stars here:** Before assessing the Spouse Palace, classical instruction requires first judging Life, Body, Fortune Virtue, Career, and Servants palaces, then coordinate with the Spouse Palace for refined reading.

**Opposite palace:** Career Palace (官祿宮) — the partner's character often reflects back on the person's professional and status environment.

---

### 4. 子女宮 Zi Nu Gong — Children Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Legacy Garden / The Creative Lineage
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 5th House (children, creativity, joy, love affairs)

**Domain:** Children, fertility, the parent-child bond, the number and gender of children, the ease or difficulty of conception and birth, the moral conduct and fortune of offspring. Also governs creative output, students, disciples, mentoring relationships, and anything produced through the generative force — including artistic and intellectual progeny.

**Beyond the surface:** In modern application, this palace speaks to all forms of creative generation and legacy transmission, not only biological children. A writer's books, a teacher's students, an artist's body of work — all fall within this palace's scope.

**Reading stars here:** Southern Dipper stars favor male children; Northern Dipper stars favor female. The Fortune Palace must be examined first: "The good/bad of this palace closely relates to lineage continuation."

**Opposite palace:** Parents Palace (父母宮) — the dynamic between these two opposite palaces reveals the full generational arc: what was received from parents and what will be transmitted to children.

---

### 5. 財帛宮 Cai Bo Gong — Wealth Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Celestial Treasury / The Abundance Ledger
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 2nd House (money, possessions, personal resources)

**Domain:** Income, earning capacity, financial approach, the methods by which wealth is generated, the stability or volatility of resources, spending patterns, and the relationship between effort and material reward.

**Beyond the surface:** A crucial classical teaching: even a dim Wealth Palace can produce adequate livelihood if the Life, Fortune Virtue, Career, and Travel palaces are strong. Conversely, a brilliant Wealth Palace means little if foundational palaces are weak. The Wealth Palace also reveals *sources* of income: steady employment (Heavenly Minister/Treasury configurations), entrepreneurial ventures (Army Breaker patterns), or speculative/irregular income (Greedy Wolf patterns).

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star with Heavenly Treasury (天府) creates exceptional wealth through financial management roles. Army Breaker (破軍) brings rapid but irregular income — boom and bust patterns.

**Opposite palace:** Career Palace (官祿宮) — Career shows how wealth is generated (the earning mechanism), while Wealth shows the flow and attitude toward what is earned. These two palaces form the most critical wealth-reading pair.

### 6. 疾厄宮 Ji E Gong — Health/Calamity Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Constitution Map / The Body's Testimony
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 6th House (health, service, daily routines, body)

**Domain:** Physical constitution, inherited body type, vulnerability to specific illnesses, the body systems most likely to face challenges, accident and injury susceptibility, recovery capacity, and — in classical interpretation — longevity range.

**Beyond the surface:** The name itself is revealing: 疾 means illness, 厄 means adversity or calamity. Modern ZWDS practitioners read it for chronic versus acute health patterns, vulnerability during specific decade cycles, and whether health crises will be self-resolving or life-altering.

**Reading stars here:** Bright favorable stars in a strong position produce robust health and strong recovery. Fire Star can suggest fever-type conditions or accidents; Yang Blade corresponds to surgeries and cuts; the Void stars suggest mysterious or difficult-to-diagnose conditions.

**Classical instruction:** "Must carefully judge three palaces: Life, Body, and Fortune Virtue, because these palaces often increase or decrease the good/bad of the Sickness Palace."

**Opposite palace:** Property Palace (田宅宮) — in the court system, the health of the body and the health of the home environment are mirror concepts.

---

### 7. 遷移宮 Qian Yi Gong — Migration/Travel Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The World Stage / The Outer Persona
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 9th House (foreign travel, higher philosophy, distant horizons) combined with 7th House public-facing quality

**Domain:** Travel, relocation, immigration, success in foreign lands, adaptation to unfamiliar environments, international business, cultural exchange, and — critically — how the person is perceived by others in public and external social contexts. This is the "outer face" that complements the "inner self" of the Life Palace.

**Beyond the surface:** The Travel Palace is the natural opposite of the Life Palace and therefore its most direct interpretive partner. Some people have their strongest stars in the Travel Palace, meaning they thrive outside their birthplace. Others have weak Travel Palaces, suggesting that stability and proximity to home is their natural flourishing condition.

**Reading stars here:** Auspicious stars indicate smooth journeys, beneficial encounters with foreigners, and success in ventures abroad. Noble stars (Tiankui, Tianyue) here often bring helpful mentors through travel connections.

**Opposite palace:** Life Palace (命宮) — must always be read together to understand the gap or alignment between private and public self.

### 8. 奴僕宮 Nu Pu Gong — Servants/Network Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Social Ecosystem / The Alliance Court / The Network of Trust
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 11th House (social networks, groups, friendships, community)

**Domain:** Subordinates, employees, domestic helpers, loyal supporters, allies of lower status who serve in supporting roles, friendships formed in adulthood, social networks, and the quality of cooperative relationships beyond family.

**Beyond the surface:** The Servants Palace reveals the quality of help a person is able to attract. A strongly configured palace here means loyal, capable subordinates and friends who show up in times of need. A weakly configured palace indicates fair-weather associates, employees who undermine, or social networks that dissolve under pressure.

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star elevates the quality of associates. Heavenly Minister (天相) or Heavenly Treasury (天府) enhance loyalty. Robbery (劫) or Void (空) configurations indicate isolation or betrayal.

**Classical instruction:** "Must assess simultaneously the brightness of stars in both the Life Palace and the Servants Palace — a dim Life Palace with a bright Servants Palace may bring helpers, but the person may not be able to make proper use of them."

**Opposite palace:** Siblings Palace (兄弟宮) — together, these two palaces map the full horizontal relationship landscape.

---

### 9. 官祿宮 Guan Lu Gong — Career/Official Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Imperial Hall / The Vocation Throne
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 10th House (career, public status, vocation, authority)

**Domain:** Career, professional achievement, the type of work one is suited to, the relationship with superiors and hierarchical authority, social status, public recognition, government affairs, and the capacity for leadership in professional contexts. The name 官祿 literally means "official salary."

**Beyond the surface:** The Career Palace is a Yang palace representing external manifestation — not just what work is done, but how the world perceives that work, and what rank or authority the person commands. Also governs legal matters connected to career, government interactions, and institutional affiliations.

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star brings natural authority; when accompanied by Left and Right Assistants, it creates patterns of genuine leadership and institutional power. Malefic stars like Yang Blade or Robbery/Void create conflicts with authority figures or professional sabotage.

**Opposite palace:** Siblings Palace (兄弟宮) — one's professional court mirrors the quality of one's peer relationships.

---

### 10. 田宅宮 Tian Zhai Gong — Property Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Ancestral Foundation / The Domain of Roots
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 4th House (home, roots, family environment, real estate)

**Domain:** Real estate, home ownership, the physical living environment, ancestral property, inherited family wealth in tangible form, land, and assets that provide material security. Also governs the quality of the home environment — whether the home is a haven of peace or a site of conflict.

**Beyond the surface:** The Property Palace speaks to the concept of "accumulated foundation" across generations. A strong palace suggests the ability to build and maintain wealth in real estate and fixed assets; it also suggests that the home environment is supportive of wellbeing.

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star or Heavenly Treasury in this palace typically indicates substantial property ownership. The interaction between this palace and the Wealth Palace is particularly important for assessing total material accumulation capacity.

**Opposite palace:** Health Palace (疾厄宮) — home environment and physical constitution mirror each other; a disrupted home often corresponds to a disrupted body.

---

### 11. 福德宮 Fu De Gong — Fortune/Virtue Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Inner Temple / The Soul Palace / The Wellbeing Reservoir
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 9th House (inner philosophy, higher meaning, spiritual life) + elements of 12th House (hidden blessings, spiritual inheritance)

**Domain:** Inner life, spiritual fortune, moral character, genuine happiness and contentment, longevity as a quality of vitality, karma and virtue accumulation, relationship with ancestors and spiritual lineage, and the capacity to find meaning even in difficulty.

**Beyond the surface:** Classical texts state that this palace "controls all 11 other palaces" — a remarkable claim that places it as the meta-palace from which all others draw their depth. A person with a strong Fortune Palace may face external hardships and still experience genuine contentment. A person with a weak Fortune Palace may accumulate wealth, status, and family — and still feel hollow. Classical texts use it to "clearly know longevity, early death, also prosperity decline, gathering dispersal of lineage."

**Reading stars here:** Purple Star produces "exceptional virtue, longevity, and spiritual authority." Malefic stars or void positions create moral struggles, shortened vitality, or difficulty accessing one's spiritual potential.

**The Siblings Palace must be examined alongside it** — the Fortune Palace's condition directly determines whether the family unit holds together.

**Opposite palace:** Parents Palace (父母宮) — what was received spiritually and morally from the family line (Parents) is transformed and expressed as inner virtue and life satisfaction (Fortune). These two palaces form a deep karmic axis.

### 12. 父母宮 Fu Mu Gong — Parents Palace

**Coined Western Name:** The Origin Gate / The Ancestral Mirror
**Western Astrology Equivalent:** 4th House (parents, family of origin) + elements of 10th House (father, authority)

**Domain:** The longevity, health, wealth, and social status of the parents; the quality of the parent-child relationship; harmony or conflict between the person and their parents; the karmic legacy carried from the family of origin; relationships with authority figures, mentors, elders, and institutions. Also governs "documents and contracts" — formal written agreements, because the parent-child bond is the primordial formal relationship.

**Beyond the surface:** The Parents Palace reveals the conditions into which a person was born. A strong palace suggests parents who are supportive, wealthy, and harmonious — the person enters life with favorable starting conditions. A weak or afflicted palace suggests early separation from parents, parental hardship, conflict with authority figures, or a family of origin that creates burdens rather than blessings.

**Reading stars here:** The Sun and Moon have special significance for parental matters regardless of which palace they occupy — their positions throughout the entire chart must be considered.

**Opposite palace:** Fortune/Virtue Palace (福德宮) — what was received from the family origin finds its final expression in the quality of the inner life. These two palaces form the full ancestral inheritance axis.

## READING INTEGRATION: KEY CROSS-PALACE DEPENDENCIES

Classical ZWDS teaching establishes mandatory multi-palace reading protocols:

- **Before reading Wealth Palace:** Must assess Life, Body, Fortune, Career, and Travel palaces first
- **Before reading Spouse Palace:** Must assess Life, Body, Fortune, Career, and Servants palaces first
- **Before reading Children Palace:** Must assess the Fortune Palace first (it governs lineage continuation)
- **Before reading Siblings Palace:** Must assess the Fortune Palace (it determines whether family gathers or disperses)
- **Before reading Health Palace:** Must assess Life, Body, and Fortune palaces (they amplify or mitigate everything)

**The Fortune Palace is described as governing all 11 other palaces** — its condition colors every other palace's expression and is arguably the most underappreciated palace in casual Western interpretations of ZWDS.

**The Life-Career-Wealth-Travel court (命財官遷)** is the primary court for overall life destiny assessment and is always read as a unit.
