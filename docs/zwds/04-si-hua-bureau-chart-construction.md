## TOPIC 1: SI HUA (四化) — THE FOUR TRANSFORMATIONS

### Origin and Conceptual Foundation

Si Hua ("four transformations") is the most dynamic and analytically powerful mechanism in ZWDS. The system arose from the integration of astrology with the I Ching (Book of Changes), allowing stars to adapt their quality based on the Heavenly Stem of whatever time-layer is being examined — natal year, decade, or annual.

The underlying metaphor is seasonal change: the same tree buds in spring, flourishes in summer, wilts in autumn, sheds in winter. A star is the tree; its Si Hua is the season imposed on it.

Each of the four transformations has its own elemental assignment in the He Tu cosmological framework:
- Hua Lu = Metal
- Hua Quan = Fire
- Hua Ke = Wood
- Hua Ji = Water

All four relate back to Earth (the Emperor archetype at the center). This governs how the transformations interact when they stack on the same star or palace.

---

### The Four Transformations — Detailed Meanings

**化禄 Hua Lu (Transform to Prosperity/Flow)**

Core qualities: smooth circulation, resources entering, abundance, flow, enjoyment, affection, talkativeness, comfort. In a palace, Hua Lu means the life domain enters a state of beneficial ease and resource arrival. It is not passive wealth — it is active flow. Think of a river channel opening up.

The classical teaching: Hua Lu represents "the acquisition of something previously absent" — it fills a need. The seasonal metaphor is Spring. Hunger (Hua Ji) creates the drive; Hua Lu is the food that satisfies it.

However, Hua Lu can also indicate over-indulgence or spending easily, since the energy is outward-flowing. In the Wealth Palace, Hua Lu is the most direct indicator of financial fortune. In the Career Palace, opportunities arrive with less friction.

**化权 Hua Quan (Transform to Power/Authority)**

Core qualities: power, authority, control, competition, action, winning, leadership, talent, independence, sometimes loneliness at the top. Hua Quan also implies amplification and duplication. The seasonal metaphor is Summer. When hunger strikes (Ji), Quan buys extra food. In a palace, it means the life domain acquires increased initiative, assertion, and control.

In the Career Palace: leadership potential, promotions, and executive authority. In the Life Palace itself: an individual who is naturally dominant and assertive. Can tip into authoritarian excess when unsupported by balancing stars.

**化科 Hua Ke (Transform to Honor/Fame)**

Core qualities: reputation, fame, elegance, academic achievement, documents, ceremonies, wisdom, politeness, refinement. In a palace, Hua Ke means the life domain gains the quality of being recognized, publicized, and respected. The seasonal metaphor is Autumn.

In the Life Palace: someone known for their learning or refinement. In the Wealth Palace: gaining wealth through reputation first — fame-driven income. In the Career Palace: academic or public-recognition paths. Hua Ke is relatively mild in force compared to Lu and Quan — it illuminates and polishes what is there.

**化忌 Hua Ji (Transform to Obstruction/Taboo)**

Core qualities: obstacles, blockage, anxiety, stubbornness, jealousy, scarcity mindset, disputes, risks. The seasonal metaphor is Winter. **Crucially, classical teaching frames Hua Ji not as pure evil but as a "void or deficiency" — hunger rather than starvation. Ji creates drive because a void demands filling.**

Hua Ji in the Life (Ming) or Body (Shen) palace means the native's early years carry difficulty, possible bodily deficiency, and a tendency toward brooding or unresolved anxiety. However, the same placement often produces unusual talent — the person who has always had to struggle finds unconventional paths. The classical phrase: 吃力不討好 — "putting in much hard work for very little visible result; a thankless task."

**The natal Hua Ji is considered the most important of all four transformations** — it is a permanent weakness point that sits in a fixed palace for life. When annual Hua Ji also flies into or clashes with the palace holding natal Hua Ji, that is the year requiring greatest caution.

### Three Layers of Si Hua

Reading only the natal Si Hua is insufficient for dynamic analysis. There are three layers:

1. **Birth-Year Si Hua** — determined by the Heavenly Stem of the birth year. Permanent baseline of the chart, the innate pattern. The natal Hua Ji in particular is a lifelong fixed pressure point.

2. **Decade Si Hua (大限四化)** — determined by the Heavenly Stem of the decade palace currently active (each decade palace has its own stem). Shows what the current 10-year period emphasizes.

3. **Annual Si Hua (流年四化)** — determined by the Heavenly Stem of the current year. Shows which door opens or closes in the specific year.

Proper analysis stacks all three: natal layer is the foundation, decade layer is the structure, annual layer is which specific door is engaged this year. When the annual Hua Ji lands on the same palace as the natal Hua Ji, or flies into that palace via a linked star, this is a critical alert period.

### Flying Stars (飞化 Fei Hua) — The Advanced Technique

"Flying stars" is the signature methodology of the northern Qin Tian school and is the key to inter-palace dynamic analysis. The core principle: every palace in the chart has a Heavenly Stem (derived from the palace's earthly branch and the chart's birth year stem). That palace's stem, applied to the Si Hua table, causes specific stars across the chart to receive transformations. Those transformed stars "fly" their energy to whatever palace they physically reside in — creating an energetic link between the sending palace and the receiving palace.

**Key rules of flying stars:**

- Flying star analysis is fundamentally about "Palace, Si-Hua, and Stars" as a trinity. Go for Palace + Si-Hua first, then add the Stars' inherent meaning.
- Any Si Hua in any palace means something, even without consulting the stars in that palace.
- Without Si Hua, stars can only be meaningfully read in the Life Self Palace and Life Spouse Palace (and in some specific cases, the Health and Career palaces). Other palace stars require Si Hua to interpret.
- All flying transformation arrows ultimately fly to Life Palaces (natal), not to decade palaces.
- **"Self-Hua" (Zi-Hua)** — when a palace's own stem transforms a star that sits in that same palace — is a special condition. In the Spouse Palace, self-Hua Ji means change in marital status.

**A worked example:** A person born in a Ren year has Tian Liang receiving Hua Lu. If Tian Liang sits in the Wealth Palace, the interpretation is that financial fortune flows through benefactor energy — money comes from helping others, through patronage. If Tian Liang sits in the Spouse Palace, the partner carries elder-sibling or protective qualities. Same transformation, completely different reading because the palace differs.

## TOPIC 2: THE BUREAU/JU SYSTEM (局)

### The Five Bureaus

There are five elemental bureaus in ZWDS, each identified by both an element and a number. The number directly encodes the starting age of the first decade luck cycle:

| Bureau | Element | Number | Decade Luck Begins at Age |
|--------|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| 水二局 | Water | 2 | Age 2 |
| 木三局 | Wood | 3 | Age 3 |
| 金四局 | Metal | 4 | Age 4 |
| 土五局 | Earth | 5 | Age 5 |
| 火六局 | Fire | 6 | Age 6 |

### What the Bureau Governs

The bureau performs two critical structural functions:

1. **Sets the starting age of the decade luck cycle (大限 Da Xian).** A Water 2 person begins their first decade of activated fortune at age 2. A Fire 6 person does not enter their first decade luck period until age 6. This means the same life palace stars may manifest their energies at meaningfully different life stages depending on bureau.

2. **Determines the position of the Zi Wei star (the Emperor Star)** — the primary anchoring star for the entire major-star system. The formula uses the lunar birth day and the bureau number to calculate how many positions to count from a starting point, placing Zi Wei in a specific earthly-branch palace. Once Zi Wei is placed, all other major stars fall into fixed positions relative to it according to established sequences.

### Bureau Calculation

The bureau is determined from birth data through a formula involving the lunar birth year's Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch, and the birth month. In manual calculation, practitioners add specific numbers derived from the lunar birth date and divide by five; the remainder maps to the bureau. Because the calculation depends on the lunar calendar, Gregorian-to-lunar conversion must be done first.

## TOPIC 3: CHART CONSTRUCTION

### The 12 Earthly Branches as Palace Positions

The structural skeleton of every ZWDS chart is the 12 Earthly Branches (地支 Di Zhi):
Zi (子), Chou (丑), Yin (寅), Mao (卯), Chen (辰), Si (巳), Wu (午), Wei (未), Shen (申), You (酉), Xu (戌), Hai (亥)

These are immovable — they do not rotate or shift. They define the 12 positions in the chart grid, arranged counterclockwise in the standard ZWDS layout.

### Locating the Life Palace (命宫 Ming Gong)

The Life Palace location is determined by two birth factors: the **lunar birth month** and the **birth hour**.

The birth hour is divided into 12 two-hour periods (时辰 Shichen), each corresponding to one Earthly Branch:

| Hour | Time | Branch |
|------|------|--------|
| Zi | 23:00–01:00 | 子 |
| Chou | 01:00–03:00 | 丑 |
| Yin | 03:00–05:00 | 寅 |
| Mao | 05:00–07:00 | 卯 |
| Chen | 07:00–09:00 | 辰 |
| Si | 09:00–11:00 | 巳 |
| Wu | 11:00–13:00 | 午 |
| Wei | 13:00–15:00 | 未 |
| Shen | 15:00–17:00 | 申 |
| You | 17:00–19:00 | 酉 |
| Xu | 19:00–21:00 | 戌 |
| Hai | 21:00–23:00 | 亥 |

**The formula for the Life Palace:** Begin at the earthly branch corresponding to the lunar birth month, then count backwards (counterclockwise) the number of positions indicated by the birth hour branch. The branch position you land on is the Life Palace.

Once the Life Palace is established, the remaining 11 palaces are placed in counterclockwise sequence:
Life → Siblings → Spouse → Children → Wealth → Health → Travel → Friends/Servants → Career → Property → Mental/Karma → Parents → back to Life

### Body Palace (身宫 Shen Gong)

Distinct from the Life Palace, the Body Palace is particularly significant for health interpretation and represents the physical manifestation of destiny. Its position is also calculated from birth month and hour through a related formula. In some charts, the Body Palace coincides with the Life Palace; in others it falls in a different palace, creating a useful polarity.

### Star Placement Process

1. **Zi Wei placed** using the bureau calculation and birth day formula — anchors the entire Northern Dipper group
2. **Tian Fu placed** based on Zi Wei's position through a fixed counter-directional sequence — anchors the Southern Dipper group
3. **14 major stars placed** in their fixed positions relative to Zi Wei and Tian Fu
4. **100+ auxiliary and minor stars placed** based on various birth data inputs: some from birth year stem and branch, some from birth month, some from birth hour
5. **Si Hua transformations placed** from the birth year's Heavenly Stem — four stars receiving Lu, Quan, Ke, and Ji marked

### Ming Zhu (命主) — Life Master Star

The Ming Zhu (Life Master Star) is determined by the Earthly Branch of the Life Palace itself — not the birth year. Each earthly branch maps to a specific ruling star, and that star becomes the Life Master: a secondary indicator of the native's life direction and character.

### Shen Zhu (身主) — Body Master Star

The Shen Zhu (Body Master Star) is determined by the Earthly Branch of the birth year — not the Life Palace branch. Each year branch maps to a ruling star for the Body Master, influencing the body's constitution and physical life trajectory.

## TOPIC 4: THREE-DIRECTION FOUR-ALIGNED COURT (三方四正)

### The Concept

San Fang Si Zheng ("three directions and four alignments") is the foundational framework for reading any single palace in context. A palace is never read in isolation — its full meaning only emerges when read in conjunction with three other specific palaces that form its "court."

### How the Courts Work

The system groups palaces in sets of four: one focal palace, two "tri-directional" companion palaces at 120-degree positions (four positions away in each direction around the 12-palace wheel), and the "opposite" palace directly across the chart (six positions away). Together these four palaces form a court.

The opposite palace is considered more influential than the two lateral companions — it exerts direct pressure and opposition/support on the focal palace.

**The four standard courts:**
- **Life Court**: Life Palace (命宫) — Wealth Palace (财帛宫) — Career Palace (官禄宫) — Friends Palace (交友宫)
- **Siblings Court**: Siblings (兄弟宫) — Health (疾厄宫) — Property (田宅宫) — Parents (父母宫)
- **Spouse Court**: Spouse (夫妻宫) — Travel (迁移宫) — Mental/Karma (福德宫) — Siblings (兄弟宫)
- **Children Court**: Children (子女宫) — Friends (交友宫) — Parents (父母宫) — Wealth (财帛宫)

The Life Court is the most important: Life, Wealth, Career, and Friends form the four-aligned court that defines a person's overall worldly trajectory.

### Practical Reading Implication

When a practitioner assesses the Career Palace, they read not only the stars and transformations in Career but also the stars and transformations landing in Life Palace, Wealth Palace, and Friends Palace. A strong Hua Lu in the Wealth Palace "opens a gate" that supports the Career reading. A Hua Ji in the Friends Palace (opposite Career) may drag on career progress through problematic subordinates or social friction.

This court-based reading principle applies across all time layers: natal, decade, and annual.

## SUMMARY: How the Four Technical Systems Interlock

The four systems interlock in a precise sequence:
1. The **Bureau** determines where Zi Wei lands and when the decade clock starts
2. The **Earthly Branches** fix the palace positions and determine the Life Palace from birth month and hour
3. The **14 major stars and 100+ auxiliaries** are placed from there
4. The birth year's **Heavenly Stem generates the four natal Si Hua**, marking which stars are transformed to Lu/Quan/Ke/Ji
5. **Flying star technique** then uses each palace's own Heavenly Stem to generate additional transformation arrows linking palaces to one another
6. **San Fang Si Zheng** provides the structural grouping — which four palaces always read together
7. **All three time-layers of Si Hua** (natal, decade, annual) are stacked for timing analysis
