Two animals, one wheel

See how your two animals read one another.

Enter two dates of birth. The Oracle scores the pair across the four Chinese systems and the Western elements, names the tier, and tells you honestly what the bond can carry. Compatibility supports a relationship. It does not make one.

Test the match

Nothing is sent anywhere. The score is drawn in your browser.

Why the score is earned

What the Oracle weighs

The pair is read across four Chinese relationships and the Western element logic. Each one moves the score, so the tier reflects a real structure rather than a guess.

Trines, San He

The twelve animals fall into four triangles of three. Animals in the same triangle share a rhythm and a way of moving through the world. A match inside a trine earns the strongest natural lift, because the pull is steady and needs little translation.

Secret friends, Liu He

Six pairs are bound as secret friends, animals that quietly complete and steady each other. The fit is easy and balancing in a way that is felt more than explained, and the Oracle weights it as highly as a trine.

Clashes, Liu Chong

Each animal sits directly across from one other on the wheel. That opposite is its clash, an energy aimed in the contrary direction. A clash is not a verdict, but it does ask for care, patience, and a willingness to translate.

Harms, Liu Hai

Six pairs carry a quieter strain, a harm that works slowly rather than loudly. Nothing breaks at once. The Oracle counts it as a small drag, the kind of friction worth naming early so it never gets to corrode.

Western elements

Each Sun sign carries Fire, Earth, Air, or Water. Shared elements feel the world the same way. Fire with Air and Earth with Water complement and feed each other. Fire with Water and Earth with Air run at different temperatures and need more bridging.

Modes

Signs also split into starters, sustainers, and shifters. Two different modes mean one person opens a thing and another keeps it alive. Two of the same mode can move as one or lock into a standoff, so the Oracle nudges the score either way.

The five tiers

What each result means

Every match lands in one of five tiers. None of them is a sentence. The highest still has to be tended, and the hardest can become the most honest bond two people ever build.

Deep Recognition

The structure is rare and almost effortless. You recognize each other early and the recognition holds. The risk in a bond this easy is that you stop tending it, sure it will hold itself. It will not. Keep choosing each other on the ordinary days and this becomes the kind of love other people quietly study. The wheel opened the door. Walking through it daily is still yours to do.

Strong

The bones are good. You move at compatible speeds and your instincts mostly agree, which means your disagreements are about the world, not about each other. Trust that foundation enough to be honest inside it. A strong match rewards directness and grows dull under politeness. Say the true thing kindly and often, and this bond deepens for years rather than coasting on its early shine.

Workable

There is real ground here and a few honest gaps. Nothing carries you automatically, which is its own gift, because a workable match teaches both people how to choose. Name where your rhythms differ and build small agreements around them. Couples in this tier often outlast the effortless ones, precisely because they learned to do on purpose what the easy pairs were handed for free.

Friction

You pull in different directions, and that tension is not the same as doom. Friction can make heat or it can make wear. The difference is whether you treat the gap as a problem to win or a difference to respect. Slow down at the edges where you grind, get specific about what each of you actually needs, and let the contrast sharpen you both rather than divide you.

Hard Teacher

This is the hard, instructive kind of bond. Something in the pairing keeps handing each of you the exact lesson you came here to learn, usually through the other person. It can wound and it can free, sometimes both. A karmic match asks for unusual honesty, firm boundaries, and the humility to grow. Held well, it changes you. Held badly, it repeats. Go in with your eyes open.

A note that holds for every tier: the score reads the shape two natures make, not the choices two people make. Attention, honesty, and repair build a relationship. The wheel only tells you what raw material you are working with.
