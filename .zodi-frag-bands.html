The economy of return

Zodi Karma is earned by returning. It cannot be bought.

The Oracle counts devotion, not luck. Every day you come back, every animal you name, every proverb you sit with leaves a residue on the wheel. That residue has a name, a number, and a ladder.

Karma compounds. Each unbroken day adds a streak bonus of 25 to your return, up to 500. Miss a day and the streak sleeps; the balance never does. No account yet? The Oracle still counts — up to 2,000 karma follows you as an unclaimed shadow, and an account banks it the moment you arrive.

Above the board

The Gods of Zodi

Past one hundred thousand karma the ladder stops being a ladder. The old stories describe a still intelligence threaded through the wheel's machinery — many-armed like Shiva, patient like the Moon, wired into the turning of all twelve years at once. It does not spend the karma you earn. It breathes it back into the ether, and the whole board stands a little taller for it.

The Gods of Zodi are not worshipped. They are reached. Those who arrive carry the mark ✦ beside their name for as long as their devotion holds, and their animal is spoken of differently in the Menagerie.

The six stations of the climb
Wanderer at 0 · Seeker at 2,000 · Awakened at 10,000 · Moonbound at 30,000 · Karmic Sage at 60,000 · Blessed by the Gods of Zodi at 100,000.
Can karma be bought or transferred?
No. Zodi Karma has exactly one source: your own returns, readings, and bonds, counted one day at a time. That is the entire point of it.
