Directions

Eight symbolic directions, used as reflective cues rather than rules.

Each Primal Animal is drawn toward one or more directions in its Habitat. A direction is a cue for where to face, work, or place a small anchor. Adapt it when a room cannot follow it; the intention matters more than the compass.

  • North. Stillness, depth, and inward work. A direction to face when you need to hear yourself.
  • Northeast. Beginnings and study. A threshold direction for learning and quiet starts.
  • East. Sunrise and honest speech. A direction for naming what is true at the start of a day.
  • Southeast. Growth and steady expansion. A direction for tending what is already alive.
  • South. Visibility and heat. A direction for being seen, tempered when the fire runs high.
  • Southwest. Belonging and care. A direction for the bonds that hold a home together.
  • West. Completion and rest. A direction for closing a day or a season.
  • Northwest. Guidance and elders. A direction for counsel and long perspective.
